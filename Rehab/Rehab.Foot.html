Rehab: Foot


Dorsiflexion

Flex foot upward, decreasing the angle between foot and shin. Can be done flat on ground, at the knee, with resistance band...

Plantar Flexion

Flex foot downward, increasing the angle between foot and shin. Can be done flat on ground, at the knee, with resistance band...

Inversion

Rotate ankle toward midline of body. There should not be any knee movement.

Eversion

Rotate ankle away from midline of body. There should not be any knee movement.

Ankle Rotations

Rotate ankle in circles as fluently as you can. There should not be any knee movement.

Alphabet Tracing

Rotate ankle in such a way to trace each letter of the alphabet. There should not be any knee movement.

Towel Curl

Place a towel under the foot. Contract the arch to curl the towel toward you. Relax and repeat.

Single Legged Balance

Stand on one foot while the other hangs in the air. No specified angle. Start at intervals of 20 seconds and move up as you see fit. Rotate ankle in such a way to trace each letter of the alphabet. You can supplement by throwing a volleyball against a wall or a medicine ball in a circle group.

Rolling the Arch

Place a tennis ball or alt at the arch. Roll vertically, horizontally, or in circles. Really press into it. Good for tightness at the metatarsals region.

Ice Rolling

Place a iced water bottle at the arch. Roll back and forth for about 8 minutes each side. Don't worry about feeling like you used too much force.
